Page 1: Gene Deitch

The Creator of Tom and Jerry

Biography:

Eugene Merril Deitch (August 8, 1924 – April 16, 2020) was an American illustrator, animator, comics artist, and film director who was based in Prague from the 1960s until his death in 2020. Deitch was known for creating animated cartoons such as Munro, Tom Terrific, and Nudnik, as well as his work on the Popeye and Tom and Jerry series.

Timeline:

  • 1924 - Born in Chicago on August 8.
    • Parents - Joseph Deitch and Ruth Delson Deitch
  • 1929 - The family moved to California, and Deitch attended school in Hollywood.
  • 1942 - Graduated from Los Angeles High School
  • After Graduation - Began working for North American Aviation, drawing aircraft blueprints.
  • 1943 - He was drafted and underwent pilot training before catching pneumonia and was honorably discharged in May of the following year.
  • (1940 - 1951) - Deitch contributed covers and interior art to the jazz magazine The Record Changer.
  • 1950 - Deitch was an early supporter and audio engineer for Connie Converse, one of the first American singer-songwriters.

Page 2: Tom and Jerry

Tom and Jerry is an American animated franchise and series of comedy short films created in 1940 by William Hanna and Joseph Barbera. Best known for its 161 theatrical short films by Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer, the series centers on the rivalry between the titular characters of a cat named Tom and a mouse named Jerry. Many shorts also feature several recurring characters.

History:

  1. Hanna-Barbera era (1940–1958)
  2. Gene Deitch era (1961–1962)
  3. Chuck Jones era (1963–1967)
  4. Second Hanna-Barbera era: The Tom and Jerry Show (1975–1977)
  5. Filmation era (1980–1982)
  6. Third Hanna-Barbera era: Tom & Jerry Kids (1990–1994)
  7. One-off productions (2001; 2005)
  8. Warner Bros. era (2006–present)
